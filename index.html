Teen Muscle Growth

To gain muscle as a teenager, focus on a consistent caloric surplus (eating more than you burn) with a diet high in protein and complex carbohydrates.

Nutritional Strategies

  • Protein (The Builder): Essential for muscle development. Aim for 30–35% of daily calories (roughly 0.8–1g per lb of body weight). Sources: Chicken, fish, eggs, dairy, beans, lean meat.
  • Carbohydrates (The Fuel): Crucial for fueling workouts. Aim for 40–50% of calories. Sources: Whole-grain pasta, rice, oats, fruits, starchy vegetables.
  • Fats (The Support): Necessary for hormone production. Aim for 20–30% of calories. Sources: Nuts, seeds, avocados, olive oil.

Execution

  • Caloric Surplus: Increase daily intake by 200–300 calories if not gaining weight.
  • Hydration: Drink water throughout the day and around workouts.
  • Consistency: Muscle growth happens over months, not days.
  • Prioritize Sleep: Muscle grows while you rest.

Disclaimer: Consult with a doctor or registered dietitian before making significant diet changes during growth phases.
