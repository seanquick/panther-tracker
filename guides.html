Nutrition Strategies

Bulk Up (Muscle Growth)

The healthiest way for a teenager to gain muscle is through a combination of consistent resistance training (3-4 times a week), consuming a slight calorie surplus (300-500 extra calories daily) focusing on whole foods, and getting 8-10 hours of sleep for recovery. Aim for slow, steady gains of 1-2 pounds per week to ensure muscle growth rather than just fat.

Nutritional Strategy

  • • Increase Calories: Consume 300–500 extra calories per day from nutrient-dense, whole foods.
  • • Prioritize Protein: (15-20%) Eat protein at every meal. Aim for 1 gram per lb of bodyweight.
  • • Fuel with Carbohydrates: (50-60%) Include oats, brown rice, potatoes, and whole-grain bread.
  • • Healthy Fats: (20-30%) Include avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil.
  • • Frequent Eating: Eat every 3–5 hours for a consistent supply of nutrients.

Be Patient: Muscle building takes time. Work with a coach to learn proper technique.

Lean Down (Healthy Weight Loss)

To lose weight healthily, teens should focus on nutrient-dense foods rather than extreme calorie cutting. A balanced approach involves ensuring a slight calorie deficit and increasing physical activity to maintain muscle.

Macro Breakdown

  • • Protein (10–30%): Crucial for muscle growth. Target 1.2–1.5 g/kg of body weight.
  • • Carbohydrates (40–60%): Focus on fiber-rich, complex carbohydrates like whole grains.
  • • Fats (25–35%): Essential for hormones. Focus on unsaturated fats (olive oil, nuts).

Key Tips: Avoid extreme diets, maintain a 20% deficit, prioritize fiber, and stay hydrated while focusing on whole foods.

It is highly recommended for teens to consult with a doctor or registered dietitian before beginning a weight loss program.
